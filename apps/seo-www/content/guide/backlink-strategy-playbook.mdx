Today, I am putting together my backlink strategy playbook for 2026, covering everything I know about back linking for SEO/GEO that we do here at Fluid Posts. We'll cover:

* Backlink analysis
* Where to get Backlinks
* Tiered link strategy
* Entity Association

Backlink matter because it's a fundamental piece in Google's Page Rank algorithm. So in theory backlinks done well = higher rankings. And since most AI systems today run a search to get the latest information, you also end up getting seen by AI systems.

In fact, in the world of AI search, the concept of back link is starting to evolve into the idea of entity association. I'll save that for the end, in the meantime, We'll get into that more at the end.

## Backlink Analysis

The first step to building links is to figure out what backlinks we need. We do this by analyzing our competitors in the space.

<div className="fd-steps">
  <div className="fd-step">
    ### Download backlink profiles
    Using a tool like Ahrefs or SEMrush, we can download the backlink profiles of us and our top 3 competitors.
  </div>
  <div className="fd-step">
    ### Clean the data
    We filter out and remove referring domains that receive zero organic traffic since those likely don't have much authority in the eyes of Google.
  </div>
  <div className="fd-step">
    ### Group the anchor text
    For each of the backlinks, we group the anchor text into one of the 7 categories:

    * Exact match: These are links that contain the exact keyword we are targeting. (backlink guide for SEO, etc.)
    * Partial match: These are links that contain the keyword we are targeting, but not exactly. (getting started with backlink guide, etc.)
    * Branded: These are links that contain our brand name. (Fluid posts, etc.)
    * Compound branded: These are links that contain our brand name and the keyword we are targeting. (Fluid posts backlink guide, etc.)
    * Generic: These are links that contain generic text. (Click here, read more, etc.)
    * Naked URL: These are links that contain the URL of the website. (https://www.fluidposts.com, etc.)
    * Image: These are links that contain an image. (These might links in the alt tags of images.)
  </div>
  <div className="fd-step">
    ### Bucket the domains
    Since not all links are the same, we bucket the domains by DR to get a rough sense of the various kinds of links that's needed. The buckets we use are:

    * DR 0 - 29
    * DR 30 - 49
    * DR 50 - 59
    * DR 60+
    
    The buckets are completely arbitrary and just a means of grouping but know that the higher the DR, the more expensive the link will likely be to get a mention in.
  </div>
  <div className="fd-step">
    ### Evaluate the link building speed
    By looking at the number of new links acquired per month for the top 3-5 competitors in the space, we can determine the natural link building speed. 
    
    This will help us determine how many links we should build per month to keep up with the competition while not getting flagged by Google.
  </div>
</div>

With all that said and done, we now have the three things:

1. The number of links in the different tiers we need to build to start outranking our competitors.
2. The anchor text distribution we need to aim for.
3. The rough number of links we can build per month.

SO now you know how many backlinks to get what the anchor text of the backlinks should say and how many backlinks you want to get per month.

## Where to get Backlinks

In general, you are trading either time or money when it comes to getting links placed.

Some common ways to get backlinks are:

1. Guest posting: You can find these sites everywhere and often just need to reach out. If you want a more done for you place, you can also consider site aggregators like [Presswhizz](https://presswhizz.com/) or [Whitepress](https://www.whitepress.com/).
2. Helping reporters: You can sign up for services like [HARO](https://www.helpareporter.com/), or [Qwoted](https://qwoted.com/), or [featured](https://featured.com/).
3. Press releases: Sites like [PR Newswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/apac/products/guaranteed-paid-placement/) or any other news syndication sites can help you get your brand name out there.

Of course, before you start these, you should first make sure that you have already establish your site's entity.

This means posting across the various places that regular business with a site do, including but not limited too:

1. Google Business Profile (if relevant)
2. Crunchbase, LinkedIn, Twitter/X, YouTube, Glassdoor, and any other platform your entity (brand) should realistically have a footprint on.
3. Major business directories (Yelp etc.)

## Tiered link strategy

So you've did the analysis, you've got the backlinks, now what? 

Well, now we just sit and wait. Things don't change overnight in SEO/GEO world unfortunately (although there are signs that [it's changing](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/juliangoldieseo_how-i-ranked-1-in-5-hours-with-perplexity-activity-7402415461339983872-yAn-/))

However, if you want to go one step further, you can build backlinks to your backlinks (backlink-ception) These are called tier 2 backlinks.

It's a way to get the most out of your expensive DR 80+ backlink that you just paid $200 for.

Before just trying to add tier 2 links for every backlink, make sure that it's: 

* Indexed
* A `do-follow` link
* On a DR50+ real domain
* Has contextual and relevant information relating to your site
* Paid or high-effort outreach

With that said, you can then use the following items to boost your backlink:

* Have posts on high authority blogging sites (Medium, SubStack, Blogger, etc.)
* Reddit/Quora/Facebook comments
* Youtube videos with backlink in the description
* Press release pointing to the backlink
* More outreach/posts on slightly lower DR sites (20-50)

This way, we make sure to extract the maximal page rank that each backlink has pointing towards us.

## Entity Association

Finally, I want to briefly cover entity association since we believe that it'll continue to get more important moving forward.

At a high level entity association is about having your site or brand being tied to a particular concept. For example, when you think fo end to end SEO/GEO automation, we ideally want you to think of Fluid Posts.

Now, to an AI, a single article mentioning this does not really lend itself to much credibility. However, having 10, 20, or even 100+ articles mentioning this link? You better bet that it'll stick.

So while the goal of backlink is to improve your site's own ranking in search. There's a nice downstream effect of serving as an AI seer, guiding the AI to mention you in ways that you would like.

## Wrapping up

To tie it altogether, make sure that you track the position of terms that you care about. Backlinks are expensive so we want to make sure that it counts.
